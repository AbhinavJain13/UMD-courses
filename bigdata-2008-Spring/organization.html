Hadoop "boot camp" (team members): an introduction to the the MapReduce programming paradigm.

  • Week 1: functional abstraction, implement toy-MapReduce in Scheme.
  • Week 2: start to work on the cluster---writing and running "Hello World" programs.
  • Week 3: more substantial programming exercises, graph algorithms.

Project management "boot camp" (team leaders): Outside speakers will be invited to share their experiences in managing research projects---including such common issues as carving large problems into smaller pieces for the team members, managing expectations, etc.

Project Work will be divided into two major phases. Each team will focus on a specific research problem.

  • The focus in Phase I is on how existing solutions to the research problem can be recast into the MapReduce framework, i.e., how can we hadoopify existing sequential algorithms?
  • The focus in Phase II is on interesting extensions, i.e., does the MapReduce framework allow us to do something that wasn't possible before?

Naturally, the exact duration of each phase will differ based on the individual project and progress; or the phases might be interwoven.

The Weekly View

Focusing on a per-week basis, here is the weekly cycle for weeks 4-12:

Monday Wednesday
Description Project meetings:
  • Team leaders and members meet to discuss progress, set goals, review experiment results, etc.
  • Team leaders given the discretion to structure this time as they see fit. Among possible options are: whiteboard sessions, discussion of readings, pair programming, demos, etc.
  • My role is to serve as facilitator and contribute substantively to the extent possible.
As necessary, I'll organize informal presentations by the groups to disseminate information relevant to all projects, e.g., tips and tricks about the infrastructure. 		External speakers will be invited to present their work. This will consist of two parts:
  • Part 1: the speaker will give a research talk (~30 minutes).
  • Part 2: the class will discuss the presented work, focusing in particular on how the research problem can be "Hadoopified".
The goal of this colloquium series is to expose students to a variety of different research problems and how MapReduce might be used to tackle them. These talks have the secondary goal of building a broader community around this technology.
Action Items
  • Team leader and each team member agree on a deliverable for the coming week. Example deliverables: writing a piece of code, running a set of experiments, deriving a model, etc.
  • Team leader and each team member assess the deliverables agreed on from the previous week.
  • Each student will write a "response piece" to reflect on the research presentation and discussion. This will be posted as a blog.
